Task 1 — Key Observations03 / 13

Observation 2

A messaging problem, not a timing problem

Response rates are completely flat all year. No month breaks out in either direction — no seasonal spike, no dip. This rules out timing or external factors as the root cause.

A consistent 1:1 ratio exists between positive responses and active rejections across every month. For every candidate who says yes, roughly one says no — a structural pattern, not coincidence.

Since all recruiters draw from the same sourcer's leads, lead quality is equal across the board. The only variable is how each recruiter writes and personalises their outreach.

Task 1 — Key Observations06 / 13

Observation 5

Lead volume is growing. Outcomes are not.

Monthly new leads — 2023

Leads grew approximately 50% from January to the August peak. Response rates stayed flat throughout the same period.

More leads are being fed into a funnel that is not improving. Without fixing the messaging and intro call conversion problems, adding volume produces more of the same at greater cost.

Before scaling lead volume further, the priority investment is recruiter capability — specifically outreach quality and intro call conversion. The sourcer is not the bottleneck.
Task 1 — Key Observations07 / 13

Observation 6

Conversion time is stable — not improving

Average time from intro call to accepted offer sits at 13.2 days with no downward trend across the year.

Summer volatility — July at 14.7 days, August at 6.9, September spiking to 18.3 — reflects holiday availability on both sides rather than any structural process issue.

Compress this timeline and throughput improves without sourcing a single additional lead.

Observation 7

1,034 rejections are a hidden pipeline

For every candidate who says yes to outreach, roughly one says no. This 1:1 ratio holds across every month of the year — it is structural, not coincidence.

The 1,034 active rejections are not cold leads. They read the message, engaged enough to respond, and made a decision. That is a far warmer pool than the 6,184 who never replied.

A re-engagement campaign with a different angle, different recruiter, or better timing could unlock meaningful pipeline without sourcing a single new lead.

Task 2 — Team Performance Assessment11 / 13

Assessment 4

Michal — wasted pipeline

1,980 leads, 4 hires, 0.2 per 100 — the lowest efficiency score on the team. 81.3% no-response rate.

Together, Fero and Michal consume 72% of all leads and produce just 23% of accepted offers.

Michal's 50% offer rejection rate is the worst on the team and warrants a separate conversation about how roles are being presented at interview stage.

Assessment 5

Anna & Peter — something to build on

Both convert at 0.5–0.6 per 100 leads — below benchmark but not without signal.

Anna's 37% intro call conversion is second best after Jana. Her problem is top-of-funnel. Fix the messaging, and her output likely moves meaningfully.

Peter's active rejection rate is the second highest after Jana — his outreach is being read. A better message framework could be the unlock.

Assessment 6

Maria — too small to judge

80 leads is not enough for firm conclusions. Her efficiency score of 2.5 looks strong — second only to Jana — but needs to hold at scale.

Her offer acceptance rate of 100% on a small sample is also encouraging, but statistically fragile.

Give Maria materially more volume now. If her numbers hold over two quarters, consider a more senior role.
Task 2 — What I Would Do Next12 / 13

Actions 1 — 3

01
Pull apart Jana's playbook and make it the team standard Document her outreach messages, intro call approach, and how she handles objections. Her outperformance holds every single day of the week — that knowledge should not stay trapped as individual know-how.
02
Use AI to operationalise Jana's style at scale Build a lightweight wrapper on an existing foundation model, pre-loaded with Jana's messages, tone, and framing. Recruiters generate first drafts without technical skill. Removes dependency on individual writing ability and scales the approach immediately.
03
Build a structured follow-up and re-engagement process 6,184 leads left unanswered, 1,034 said no — with no follow-up track behind either group. Every lead should get 2–3 follow-ups before being marked inactive. The 1,034 active rejections need their own track: different angle, different recruiter, or different timing.
